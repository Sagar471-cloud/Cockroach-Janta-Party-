What is CJP? Background

Origin

On 15 May 2026, Chief Justice Surya Kant compared unemployed youth and activists to "cockroaches and parasites of society." Instead of being silenced, India's Gen Z flipped the insult. Within 78 hours, the Cockroach Janta Party had 3 million Instagram followers. Within one week — 20 million, more than double the ruling BJP's audience.

Founder

Abhijeet Dipke, 30, a political communications strategist who previously worked with the Aam Aadmi Party. He built the website using AI tools including Claude and ChatGPT. The CJP is not a registered political party — it is a satirical protest movement demanding accountability.

Who can join?

Eligibility: be "lazy," "unemployed," or "chronically online." No religion check, no caste check, no gender check. The tagline: "Voice of the Lazy & Unemployed."

Why is the govt scared?

On 21 May 2026, MeitY (Ministry of Electronics & IT) ordered CJP's X (Twitter) account withheld in India under Section 69A of the IT Act, citing "national security." The Intelligence Bureau claimed CJP content was "inflammatory." Opposition leaders called it a blatant attack on free speech.

1. Judiciary independence — no post-retirement rewards

No Chief Justice shall be given a Rajya Sabha seat, governorship, or any government post after retirement. The CJI who called youth "cockroaches" was widely seen as friendly to the ruling establishment. CJP demands a clean break between judiciary and government patronage.

2. Electoral accountability — protect every vote

If any legitimate vote is deleted or tampered with, the Chief Election Commissioner must be arrested under UAPA — because destroying voting rights is "no less than terrorism." This demand arose from the 2025 Indian electoral controversy where lakhs of voter deletions were reported.

3. Women's reservation — 50%, not 33%

The Women's Reservation Act passed in 2023 gives only 33% seats. CJP demands 50% — without increasing the total strength of Parliament. Additionally, 50% of all Cabinet positions must be reserved for women. Protest rallies specifically on this demand have been held in Delhi, Mumbai, and Chennai.

Women's Rights33% → 50% Demand

4. Independent media — cancel Ambani & Adani licenses

CJP's manifesto explicitly names Mukesh Ambani (Network18, TV18, CNN-News18) and Gautam Adani (NDTV) as owners of India's major news channels. It demands cancellation of all media licenses held by companies owned by these two billionaires, and creation of a truly independent public broadcasting system.

Press FreedomMedia Monopoly

5. Full financial transparency — where did the money go?

CJP's vision statement: "We are not here to set up another PM CARES, holiday in Davos on taxpayer salary, or rebrand corruption as strategic spending. We are here to ask — loudly, repeatedly, in writing — where the money went." Full RTI compliance, mandatory audit of PM CARES, and public disclosure of all electoral funding.

Dissolve NTA — fix the exam system

After NEET 2024 (24L affected), UGC-NET cancellation (9L affected), and UP Police exam (48L affected) — CJP demands complete dissolution of NTA and creation of an independent, transparent examination authority with zero tolerance for paper leaks. Protesters in cockroach costumes demonstrated outside NTA offices.

Solve youth unemployment — 40% graduates jobless

Azim Premji University found 40% of graduates under 25 are unemployed. CJP demands a National Youth Employment Mission, regulation of AI displacement of entry-level jobs, and annual public reporting of real employment data — not adjusted government statistics.

Repeal sedition-era internet laws

Section 69A of the IT Act was used to block CJP's own X account within days of its founding. CJP demands repeal or fundamental reform of Section 69A, 66A (already struck down but still used by police), and all laws used to silence political dissent online.

End caste-based discrimination in hiring

The movement demands strict enforcement of anti-discrimination laws in private sector hiring, mandatory diversity reporting for companies above 500 employees, and expansion of reservation benefits to include economic criteria alongside social ones.

NEET-UG 2024 — 24 lakh students affected

The country's largest medical entrance exam was compromised. Paper leaked in Bihar and Rajasthan. Grace marks controversy followed. Supreme Court ordered CBI probe. NTA's credibility collapsed. CJP protest outside NTA HQ drew thousands. Students' careers destroyed overnight.

UGC-NET June 2024 — cancelled 1 day after exam

Paper was leaked on the dark web the same night as the exam. 9 lakh candidates who had already appeared were told it was cancelled. Ministry announced NTA restructuring. CJP demanded complete dissolution of NTA.

Cancelled9L CandidatesNTA Restructure

UP Police Constable 2024 — India's largest single cancellation

48 lakh candidates. Exam cancelled due to paper leak. The single largest exam cancellation in Indian history. Re-exam conducted months later. Accused arrested included serving policemen — the very people meant to prevent crime.

48L CandidatesLargest EverPolice Involved

RRB-NTPC 2021 — trains blocked, Bihar shutdown

Students set fire to trains and blocked rail lines across Bihar after result manipulation and paper leak allegations. Government ordered re-exam. High court intervened. This was an early warning of exam system rot that was ignored for 3 more years.

BPSC 70th CCE 2024 — students still protesting

Bihar Public Service Commission exam embroiled in paper leak and mass cheating. Students in Patna sat in protest dharna for weeks. Many were lathi-charged. CJP adopted this as one of its core campaigns alongside NEET.

Why AI makes it worse

India's back-office IT industry (BPO, KPO) employs millions of young graduates in entry-level roles. Generative AI is now automating these exact tasks. A 2025 survey found 54% of Indian Gen Z have postponed buying homes and 44% of millennials have delayed marriage due to economic uncertainty. The jobs pipeline is narrowing while graduates multiply.

A National Youth Employment Mission with legally binding targets. Mandatory annual reporting of employment data verified by an independent body (not NSSO alone). Tax incentives for companies that hire and retain graduates for minimum 2 years. AI disruption impact assessment before large-scale automation deployment in any sector employing more than 10,000 people.
